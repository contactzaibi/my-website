Final Year Research

Academic Research

Econometric investigation of innovation patterns in Pakistani enterprises.

BS Economics Thesis · 2025–2026

The Nexus of R&D, Absorptive Capacity, and Innovation: Evidence from Pakistani Enterprises

Supervisor
Dr. Muhammad Zeeshan Younas
Lecturer, Applied Economics & Research Methods
GCUF Affiliated Govt. Graduate College
Dataset
World Bank Enterprise Survey 2015
696 Pakistani firms
Key Finding

R&D investment alone yields statistically insignificant innovation outcomes. Returns become significant only when moderated by employee training (β = 0.130, p < 0.01) and process upgrades (β = 0.230, p < 0.01) — demonstrating that human capital and operational readiness are co-requisites, not optional additions, for R&D-driven innovation in Pakistani enterprises.
